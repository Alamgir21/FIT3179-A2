Tourism in Australia

Australia is home to a wide variety of cultures and is a popular destination for tourists from around the world. The data presented here highlights the vast variety of travellers that visit Australia each year, as well as the significant contribution that tourism makes to the Australian economy. Below Tourism density is used to normalise the data. It is simply calculated by dividing the total number of tourists from each country by the total land mass of that country.

This allows us to see which countries have the highest proportion of their population visiting Australia, rather than just looking at the raw number of visitors. For example, while China may have a high number of visitors to Australia, its large land mass means that the proportion of Chinese people visiting Australia is relatively low compared to smaller countries with fewer visitors but also smaller landmasses. Landmass was used instead of population since population data changes over time but landmass remains constant in most cases.

As seen above, the summer period from December to February is the peak season for international visitors to Australia. This is likely due to a combination of factors, including the holiday season in many countries, favorable weather conditions in Australia during this time, and school holidays.

The data also shows a significant drop in international visitors during the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, which is expected due to travel restrictions and border closures implemented to curb the spread of the virus. Overall, this graph provides insight into the seasonal patterns of international tourism in Australia and highlights the impact of external factors such as the pandemic on travel trends.

How is tourism on a regional level?

Tourism within the Australian states varies significantly, with some states attracting far more international visitors than others. This could be due to a variety of factors, such as the presence of popular tourist attractions, accessibility, and population size. For example, New South Wales and Victoria are home to major cities like Sydney and Melbourne, which are popular destinations for tourists. In contrast, states like Tasmania and the Northern Territory may have fewer international visitors due to their smaller populations and more remote locations.

Some common tourist attractions in Australia include the Great Barrier Reef, Sydney Opera House, Uluru, and the beaches of the Gold Coast. Additionally, states with diverse landscapes and outdoor activities, such as Queensland and Western Australia, may also attract more international visitors. Overall, the distribution of international visitors across Australian states highlights the importance of various factors in shaping tourism patterns.

What is tourisms impact on the Australian economy?

Tourism play a much smaller role in the overall economy. Not to say it is insiginifanct however, as seen in the pie chart above, tourism contributes to around 3.1% of Australia's GDP in 2023-24. This is a significant increase from previous years, likely due to the recovery of the tourism industry following the COVID-19 pandemic. The pie chart also shows that domestic tourism makes up a larger portion of the tourism GDP compared to international tourism. This highlights the importance of domestic travel in supporting the Australian economy. Overall, while tourism may not be the largest contributor to Australia's GDP, it still plays a vital role in the country's economy and provides employment opportunities for many Australians.

Education and training, food and sports services are among the top employment sectors within the tourism industry in Australia. These sectors are essential to providing a positive experience for tourists, as they encompass a wide range of services that cater to the needs and interests of visitors. The Education sector is almost 2x the next highest sector, food establishments.

The job market in the tourism industry has seen significant growth over the past decade, with the number of jobs increasing from around 500,000 in 2016-17 to over 700,000 in 2023-24. This growth can be attributed to several factors, including the recovery of the tourism industry following the COVID-19 pandemic and the overall growth of the Australian economy.
